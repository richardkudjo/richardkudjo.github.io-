Wilmar Africa — Regional Automation Program

2018 — 2024  |  West and North Africa  |  14 Facilities
AOMS Deployed ICM Deployed Audited Outcomes ISO 13374

This is the primary field validation of the AOMS and ICM frameworks. Across 14 food and beverage manufacturing facilities, both frameworks were deployed, refined, and sustained over a six-year operating record. All performance data was measured and reported through Finance Department audits and external auditors, as independently confirmed by the Regional Technical Head who supervised the work directly.

The deployment covered PLC and SCADA integration, predictive maintenance implementation, energy management, power quality optimization, worker safety systems, and workforce training. The platforms used are the identical platforms dominant in U.S. food manufacturing: Siemens S7-1500, Allen-Bradley ControlLogix, and OSIsoft PI Historian. The standards applied are the same standards governing U.S. food manufacturing automation.

267 work orders were managed during an 18-month multi-site validation period, with a 96.25% completion rate and 99.67% budget accuracy. These figures were independently documented in operational records.

Technology Stack

Platforms Deployed Across All Projects

These are not legacy or regional platforms. They are the identical systems dominant in U.S. food manufacturing, as independently confirmed by engineering experts with direct U.S. and Canadian food industry experience.

PLC Systems

Siemens S7-1500 & Allen-Bradley ControlLogix / CompactLogix

The two dominant PLC platforms in U.S. food manufacturing. Deployed and configured across all 14 primary network facilities. Certifications held for both platforms.

SCADA

Siemens WinCC

Supervisory control and data acquisition systems integrated with the S7-1500 PLC architecture. Used for real-time monitoring, alarm management aligned to ANSI/ISA-18.2-2016.

Historian

OSIsoft PI Historian

Industrial data historian providing the continuous operational data stream that feeds AOMS predictive diagnostics and generates the audit trail required for FSMA preventive control documentation.

Energy Monitoring

Schneider Electric Power Logic

Energy metering and power quality monitoring integrated across all facilities. Provided the real-time data foundation for the power factor correction work that eliminated $450,000 in annual utility demand penalties.

U.S. Application

Why These Results Transfer Directly
to U.S. Food Manufacturing

The 14-facility network where AOMS and ICM were validated operated under constrained infrastructure and limited capital. U.S. food manufacturing facilities run on the same platforms, under the same international standards, but with more stable infrastructure and greater capital access. The documented outcomes represent a conservative baseline for what is achievable in U.S. SMFMEs. Applied across even a modest share of the more than 80% of U.S. food processing establishments that are small and medium-sized enterprises, these performance improvements translate into billions of dollars in annual savings, measurable reductions in the sector's 60,000 annual workplace injuries, and direct progress toward FDA FSMA compliance and DOE energy efficiency targets.

PHASE 01

Pilot Deployment

Predictive maintenance and energy optimization modules deployed at select U.S. facilities to establish baseline performance improvements under U.S. operating conditions.

PHASE 02

Network Expansion

Full AOMS and ICM deployment across additional facilities, incorporating workforce training, FSMA compliance documentation integration, and system standardization.

PHASE 03

Sector Dissemination

Peer-reviewed publications, industry collaboration, and engagement with NIST MEP and Manufacturing USA programs enabling broad adoption across SMFMEs nationwide.
