Proprietary Frameworks

AOMS & ICM

Two frameworks developed from 15 years of field practice across 28 industrial projects. Both are peer-reviewed, field-tested, and designed to address specific, measurable gaps in U.S. industrial infrastructure.

Framework 01
01
Automated Operations Management System
AOMS
Predictive Maintenance in Food Processing Plants — Field-Validated Framework Based on ISO 13374 Condition Monitoring Architecture

AOMS is a predictive maintenance framework structured around ISO 13374 Parts 1 through 4, incorporating ISO 20816-3:2022, IEEE 1415-2006, ANSI/ISA-18.2-2016, and ISA-TR18.2.3-2024. It integrates Industrial IoT sensor networks with machine learning diagnostics to detect equipment degradation before failure occurs, converting reactive maintenance schedules into data-driven, condition-based interventions specifically validated for food processing environments.

AOMS was developed and validated across 28 manufacturing facilities in 13 countries, including 14 food and beverage manufacturing plants within a single multinational network. It does not require full infrastructure replacement. It layers onto existing PLC and SCADA systems, making it practical for U.S. small and medium-sized food manufacturing enterprises (SMFMEs) that cannot afford complete system overhauls.

The Problem AOMS Solves

The Problem

Unplanned Downtime Costs U.S. Manufacturers Billions Annually

Equipment failures in food processing plants are rarely random. They follow detectable patterns. Without real-time monitoring, plants rely on fixed maintenance schedules that either over-service equipment unnecessarily or miss failures entirely. The result is lost production, spoiled product, and regulatory exposure.

The Solution

AOMS Detects Failure Before It Happens

By placing IIoT sensors at critical process points and feeding that data into trained ML diagnostic models, AOMS identifies anomalies 48 to 72 hours before they become failures. Maintenance teams receive prioritized alerts, allowing targeted interventions rather than full-line shutdowns.

How AOMS Works

STEP 01

Sensor Integration

IIoT sensors attach to critical process equipment. They continuously stream vibration, temperature, pressure, and flow data to a central analytics layer.

STEP 02

ML Diagnostics

Machine learning models trained on equipment baseline data analyze incoming sensor streams in real time, flagging deviations that indicate early-stage degradation.

STEP 03

Prioritized Alerts

Maintenance teams receive ranked alerts with failure probability scores, expected time-to-failure estimates, and recommended interventions. No false-alarm overload.

Framework 02
02
Integrated Competitiveness Model
ICM
Bridging Industry 4.0 and Industry 5.0 in Manufacturing

ICM is a six-domain competitiveness model that gives manufacturing operators a structured, practical path to digital transformation. It bridges the gap between Industry 4.0 automation and the human-centric principles of Industry 5.0, without requiring a complete infrastructure overhaul.

Most digital transformation frameworks are designed for large enterprises with dedicated IT departments and large capital budgets. ICM was built for the operational reality of U.S. small and mid-size manufacturers, where budget constraints, legacy equipment, and workforce limitations are the norm. It provides a domain-by-domain assessment and improvement roadmap that plants can implement incrementally.

The Problem ICM Solves

The Problem

Most U.S. SME Manufacturers Are Stuck Between Industry 4.0 and 5.0

Industry 4.0 adoption in U.S. manufacturing has been uneven. Small and mid-size plants often have partial automation, disconnected data systems, and no clear roadmap for what comes next. Industry 5.0 adds new demands around human-machine collaboration and sustainability. The gap between where most plants are and where they need to be is wide and growing.

The Solution

ICM Maps the Gap and Defines the Steps

ICM assesses a plant across six operational domains, scores each domain against an Industry 5.0 readiness benchmark, and produces a prioritized improvement roadmap. Each domain has defined entry points for both legacy and modern infrastructure, so plants at any maturity level can start immediately.

The Six Domains

DOMAIN 01

Smart Automation

Integration of the AOMS predictive maintenance framework and IIoT systems to automate condition monitoring, fault detection, and maintenance scheduling across production equipment.

DOMAIN 02

Energy Management

Power quality optimization, power factor correction, and real-time energy monitoring aligned with ISO 50001. Validated outcomes include $1.25 million in annual energy savings and a 15.5% reduction in electricity consumption per unit produced.

DOMAIN 03

Safety and Compliance

Remote operation systems for medium-voltage switchgear, arc flash elimination, and FSMA preventive control documentation aligned with 21 CFR Parts 117 and 507. AOMS audit trail architecture directly satisfies FDA FSMA documentation requirements.

DOMAIN 04

Process Control

Multi-site PLC and SCADA integration using Siemens S7-1500, Allen-Bradley ControlLogix, and OSIsoft PI Historian. Standardized control architecture reduces operating costs and ensures consistency across facilities.

DOMAIN 05

Implementation and Training

Structured workforce development covering PLC programming, SCADA systems, vibration analysis, and alarm management. Over 60 engineers and technicians trained across multiple countries, with skills sustained after initial deployment.

DOMAIN 06

Operational Technology Cybersecurity

OT security architecture aligned to ANSI/ISA-62443-2-1-2024, Digital Twin integration per ISO/IEC 30186:2025, and Agentic AI guardrails per IEC 61508. Directly supports the MSc thesis research on explainable AI for SCADA cybersecurity.
